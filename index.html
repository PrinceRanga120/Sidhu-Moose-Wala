Life & Legacy

In Memoriam

Shubhdeep Singh Sidhu (Sidhu MooseWala)

Born: June 11, 1993 — Moosa, Mansa District, Punjab, India
Died: May 29, 2022 (aged 28) — Jawaharke, Mansa District, Punjab, India

Cause of Death: Assassination by gunshot. On the evening of May 29, 2022, Sidhu MooseWala was shot dead by unidentified assailants while driving his car in Jawaharke village. The attack was linked to inter-gang rivalry. His death sent shockwaves across the globe, triggering an outpouring of grief from millions of fans, fellow artists, and political figures. He remains immortal in the hearts of Punjabis worldwide.

Sidhu MooseWala — born Shubhdeep Singh Sidhu — was not merely a singer; he was a cultural earthquake that redefined Punjabi music forever. Hailing from the small village of Moosa in Mansa, Punjab, he grew up in a humble farming family. His father, Balkaur Singh, and mother, Charan Kaur, provided a grounded upbringing that would later reflect deeply in his lyrics rooted in village life, pride, and raw emotion.
After completing his schooling in Mansa, Sidhu moved to Ludhiana for higher studies and eventually pursued Electrical Engineering at the Guru Nanak Dev Engineering College. It was during his college years that the spark of music ignited. He began writing lyrics as "Shubhdeep" and adopted the stage name "MooseWala" as a tribute to his native village.
His breakthrough came with "So High" (2017), a track produced by Byg Byrd, which exploded across Punjab and the diaspora. The song's gritty realism, combined with Sidhu's deep baritone voice, created a new genre — "Sidhu MooseWala Music." He followed this with a relentless stream of hits: "Issa Jatt," "Tochan," "Famous," "Dollar," "Legend," "Old Skool," "Same Beef," "295," "The Last Ride," and countless others.
Sidhu wasn't just a musician; he was a poet, actor, politician, and voice of the youth. He made his acting debut in "Yes I Am Student" and later entered politics, joining the Indian National Congress and contesting the 2022 Punjab Legislative Assembly election from Mansa. Though he lost, his influence on youth politics was undeniable.
His album "Moosetape" (2021) shattered every streaming record in Indian music history. With 32 tracks, it became the most-streamed album by an Indian artist on Spotify in the first 24 hours. His posthumous releases continue to dominate charts, proving that legends never die — they simply become timeless.

MOOSETAPE — The Crown Jewel

Released on May 15, 2021, Moosetape is Sidhu MooseWala's magnum opus — a 32-track masterpiece that redefined Punjabi music globally. Produced primarily by The Kidd and Snappy, the album blends raw street narratives with melodic depth, featuring no commercial compromises.

The album debuted at #65 on Billboard Canadian Albums and shattered streaming records, becoming the most-streamed album by an Indian artist on Spotify in the first 24 hours. Tracks like "295," "US," "Brown Shortie," "Signed to God," "Burberry," and "The Last Ride" became anthems.
